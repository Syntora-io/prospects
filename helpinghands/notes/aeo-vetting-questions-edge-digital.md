## Questions to Ask

### 1. "Are you doing anything right now specifically for AI search -- like ChatGPT, Perplexity, or Google's AI Overviews?"
Why this matters: This separates agencies that understand AEO from those still only doing traditional SEO. If they say "yes, our SEO covers that" -- that's a red flag. AI search uses completely different ranking signals than Google organic.

### 2. "When I search for a cleaning service in Naperville on ChatGPT, we don't show up. What would you do to fix that?"
Why this matters: Forces a specific, actionable answer. Vague responses like "we'll optimize your content" aren't enough. A good answer would mention structured data, directory listings, content formatting for AI extraction, or dedicated AI-targeted pages.

### 3. "Do you know what robots.txt rules are needed for AI bots? Like GPTBot or PerplexityBot?"
Why this matters: Most SEO agencies only think about Googlebot. AI engines have their own crawlers (GPTBot, PerplexityBot, ClaudeBot, GoogleOther) that need to be explicitly allowed in robots.txt. If they don't know what these bots are, they haven't done AEO work.

### 4. "Have you set up an llms.txt file on our site?"
Why this matters: llms.txt is a newer standard that gives AI models a structured summary of what your business does. AIOSEO Pro can auto-generate one, but it's usually generic boilerplate. A good agency would know about this and have customized it.

### 5. "How do you measure whether AI assistants are recommending us? Do you have any reports showing that?"
Why this matters: Traditional SEO reporting shows Google rankings, clicks, impressions. AEO requires tracking citations across multiple AI engines -- ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, Claude, etc. If they can't show AI-specific reporting, they aren't measuring it.

### 6. "Are you creating any content specifically for AI bots to read -- not for people visiting the website?"
Why this matters: AEO pages are structured differently from SEO pages. They use direct question-answer format in the first two sentences, are hidden from site navigation, and are submitted through separate sitemaps. If they say "all our content is for both" -- they don't understand the difference.

### 7. "What structured data or schema markup do we have on the site right now? Is there FAQ schema on our service pages?"
Why this matters: AI engines rely heavily on schema markup (JSON-LD) to understand businesses. Helping Hands currently has Organization, ProfessionalService, and BreadcrumbList schema -- but is missing Service (per service type), FAQ, AggregateRating, and HowTo. A good agency would know exactly what's there and what's missing.

### 8. "How many pages are you creating for us each month, and how do you decide what to write about?"
Why this matters: Scale matters for AEO. A good AEO strategy produces dozens to hundreds of pages per week targeting specific question-answer combinations across all your service areas. If they're doing 2-4 blog posts a month, that's traditional content marketing -- not AEO.

### 9. "Are we listed on directories that AI specifically pulls from -- like Crunchbase, Wikidata, or data aggregator sites?"
Why this matters: AI engines pull business information from different sources than Google. Traditional directories (Yelp, Angi, HomeAdvisor) matter, but AI models also reference tech/data platforms, structured knowledge bases, and aggregator APIs. If Edge Digital is only thinking about traditional directories, there's a gap.

### 10. "What's your plan for making sure we show up when someone asks Google's Gemini for a cleaning service recommendation?"
Why this matters: Google's AI Overviews and Gemini are taking over the top of search results. This is the most important near-term shift. Gemini pulls heavily from Google Maps data (100% profile accuracy correlation) and YouTube content (0.737 correlation -- higher than backlinks). A strong answer mentions Google Business Profile optimization, YouTube content strategy, and structured data.

## How to Use These

1. You don't need to ask all 10. Pick the ones you're most comfortable with.
2. Questions 1, 2, and 5 are the most important -- they quickly reveal if Edge Digital is doing real AEO work.
3. Don't explain what AEO is before asking. Let them demonstrate their knowledge.
4. If they ask where you heard about this, you can say "a technology consultant I'm working with."
5. Record the whole call -- Parker will review the transcript and give you a straight answer on where they stand.

## What Good Answers Sound Like

**Strong signals (they get it):**
- They can name specific AI bots (GPTBot, PerplexityBot, ClaudeBot)
- They mention separate content strategies for AI vs human readers
- They talk about structured data beyond basic Organization schema
- They have some form of AI citation tracking or share-of-voice measurement
- They mention question-answer content formats designed for AI extraction
- They reference Google AI Overviews, Perplexity, or ChatGPT by name with specific tactics

**Weak signals (traditional SEO only):**
- "Our SEO strategy covers AI too"
- "We're keeping an eye on AI developments"
- "We focus on quality content that works for everyone"
- They can't name any AI-specific bots or crawlers
- No AI citation measurement or reporting
- Content volume is a handful of blog posts per month
- They only mention Google rankings and organic traffic metrics
